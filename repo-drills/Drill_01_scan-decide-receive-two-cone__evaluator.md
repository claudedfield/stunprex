## What this drill does best (the two strongest things)

1. **The design is the constraint.** The drill doesn't ask the coach to enforce the scan with shouting — it engineers the environment so that *the scan is the only way to know what the touch should do*. This is the cleanest possible expression of Conviction 13 (constraints generate creativity) applied to the design method itself. Every subsequent drill in the library should aspire to this property: the desired behaviour is the easy path; the violating behaviour is the hard path. This is the gold-standard property to preserve.

2. **The Capacities Framework tagging is rigorous, not decorative.** Primary and secondary capacities are distinguished by family per Codex Part II; affective and adaptive are correctly named as multipliers and progression-seeds rather than as primary teaching targets. The drill is searchable by capacity gap (a coach who has identified a player's selective-attention weakness will find this drill); the library structure depends on this being done well from Drill 01 forward. It is.

## One thing to watch on Drill 02 (Cone Sea, methodology-only)

**Decision E lock from Plan v1.1:** Sequence Boards are out of Q1 and pushed to Wave 3. Cone Sea ships Wk 9 as *methodology only* — no Sequence Boards CTA, no implication that the boards exist yet, no "coming soon" hook that creates a content-product debt the team can't yet pay.

Drill 02's risk is that the Codex's worked example for Cone Sea explicitly names "Cone Sea (with Sequence Boards)" as a four-family drill (perceptual + cognitive + motor + adaptive). The Drill Architect must produce a *standalone Cone Sea* drill that delivers the perceptual + cognitive + motor layers without the Sequence Boards — and must not write the drill as if the boards are missing or pending. The drill must stand on its own pedagogical merit, not as a Boards-shaped void.

Recommended brief for Drill 02:
- Title: *"Cone Sea"* (no parenthetical Sequence-Boards qualifier).
- Capacities trained: three families (perceptual + cognitive + motor), with adaptive seeded at Level 4–5 via surface and call variants.
- No reference to Sequence Boards anywhere in the drill — not even as "Note: a Boards version exists." The boards are out of Q1; drill text should not anchor reader expectation to a future product.
- Same structural rigour as Drill 01: 5 progressions, ≥3 variations, full coach guidance, parent-facing note, refusals.
